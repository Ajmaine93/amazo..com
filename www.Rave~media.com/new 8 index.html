*Step 8- select trim size

*Trim size

Trim size describes a book page after excess material required in production has been cut off to its desired width and height from a larger size: the more words per page, the fewer pages overall. It also determines the design and layout fitness ine the book page.

*bleed or No Bleed

Bleed is a printing term that refers to when printed items on a page, such as images or illustrations, reach all the way to the edge of the page.
No bleed is when the text, lines or images don't go all the way to the edge of the paper and there is a white border all the way around the edge of the page

*margins

Every page has three outside margins (top, bottom, and side) and one inside margin (also called the gutter). Margins ensure your text isn’t cut off during manufacturing. Set your trim size before you set your margins, since margin size depends on page count and on whether you have elements that bleed.

*Glossy and Matte

Glossy finish is shiny. It makes black covers darker and artwork more striking. It's typical for textbooks, children's books, and nonfiction.
Matte finish has minimal sheen and a subtle, polished look. It's typical for novels and other fiction.

#4. Pricing rights and Royalty option

*Territories

Choose “All territories (worldwide rights)” unless you have a good reason not to.
This will make your book available in all of Amazon’s global territories once you’re ready to publish it.

*Primary market place

Choose Amazon.com as your primary marketplace, then set the list price. The price in other marketplaces will be set according to exchange rates, but you can set each one manually if you want to.

*Pricing and royalty.

The pricing is always up to the Author of the book, you will be given a price range to select depending on what your book format worths, This is a very fragile stage, pricing your book to a large cost without a good or attrative content will most definately drive buyers away, so price responsibly.
